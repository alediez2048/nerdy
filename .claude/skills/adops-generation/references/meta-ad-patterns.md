# Meta Ad Patterns — What Works Right Now

Reference patterns for the reference-decompose-recombine pipeline.

## Hook Types

### Question Hook
> "Is your child's SAT score holding them back?"

- Works for: Parent audiences, awareness campaigns
- Why: Creates curiosity gap, invites self-identification
- Best paired with: problem-agitate-solution body

### Stat Hook
> "Students who prep score 200+ points higher on average."

- Works for: Both audiences, conversion campaigns
- Why: Specific numbers build credibility immediately
- Best paired with: stat-context-offer body

### Story Hook
> "My daughter went from a 1050 to a 1400 in 8 weeks."

- Works for: Parent audiences, both campaign types
- Why: Social proof in narrative form, relatable
- Best paired with: testimonial-benefit body

### Fear Hook
> "The SAT is 3 months away. Is your student ready?"

- Works for: Parent audiences, conversion campaigns
- Why: Creates urgency via time pressure
- Best paired with: problem-agitate-solution body
- **Compliance note:** Must not imply the child is deficient

## Body Patterns

### Problem → Agitate → Solution → Proof → CTA
1. Name the pain point
2. Make it feel real/urgent
3. Present Varsity Tutors as the solution
4. Back it up (stat, testimonial, social proof)
5. Clear next step

### Testimonial → Benefit → CTA
1. Quote or paraphrase a success story
2. Connect to the specific benefit
3. Clear next step

### Stat → Context → Offer → CTA
1. Lead with a compelling number
2. Explain why it matters
3. Present the offer
4. Clear next step

## CTA Patterns (Match Funnel Stage)

| Funnel Stage | CTA | Why |
|-------------|-----|-----|
| Awareness | "Learn More" | Low commitment, information-seeking |
| Consideration | "See How It Works" | Mid-funnel, curiosity |
| Conversion | "Start Free Practice Test" | Specific, free, low-friction |
| Conversion | "Book Your Free Session" | Action-oriented, free trial |
| Conversion | "Get Your Score Report" | Value-first, lead capture |

## What Converts on Meta

- Specific numbers ("200+ point improvement") > vague promises ("better scores")
- Social proof (reviews, ratings, student counts) > claims
- Urgency (deadlines, limited spots) > open-ended offers
- Free trials/assessments > paid commitments as first step
- Authentic/UGC-style > polished/corporate
- First line stops the scroll — everything else is secondary
